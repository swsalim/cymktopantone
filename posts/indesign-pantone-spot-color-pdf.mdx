InDesign is the bridge between **layout** and **print production**. If Pantone is specified on the estimate but your PDF only contains **process CMYK builds**, you can still get a decent print—but you will not get a true **spot ink** run unless the file, the swatches, and the export settings all agree.

This article is for the common search: **“How do I set up Pantone in InDesign and export a PDF the printer will actually separate as PMS?”**

## Start with the printer’s language

Before you touch Swatches, confirm:

- **Which Pantone book?** Coated (**C**) and uncoated (**U**) are different specs.
- **Spot or sim?** Some jobs use **Pantone + CMYK** (logo spot, photos process). Others simulate Pantone with **process builds** only.
- **PDF version and output intent** your vendor prefers (often **PDF/X** variants).

Write that into the **job folder** and the **first page** of the PDF if your studio uses slug lines—future you will thank present you.

## Add a Pantone color as a **spot** swatch

1. Open **Window → Color → Swatches**.
2. From the Swatches panel menu, choose **New Color Swatch…** (or the **+** icon, depending on version).
3. Set **Color Type** to **Spot** (not Process).
4. For **Color Mode**, choose **PANTONE + Solid Coated** (or the book your printer named). Pick the exact **PMS number** from the list.

**Tip:** Name the swatch exactly how the brand book names it (`PANTONE 293 C`). Consistency beats clever abbreviations when a prepress operator searches the PDF.

If Pantone lists do not appear in your Adobe build, you may need **Pantone Connect** or an updated library package. While IT sorts that out, you can still **derive a working HEX or CMYK** from the brand guide, then run <Link href="/convert-hex-to-pantone-pms">HEX to Pantone</Link> or <Link href="/convert-cmyk-to-pantone-pms">CMYK to Pantone</Link> to confirm the **closest PMS** before you manually assign the swatch name the client expects.

## Apply spot color to text and frames

- **Text:** Select characters or paragraphs, then choose the **spot swatch** as the **character color**.
- **Frames:** Select the frame, set **Fill** or **Stroke** to the spot swatch.
- **Images:** Spot color usually does **not** replace a full-color image. For duotones or spot channels, use **Object → Image Color Settings** or prepared TIFF/PSD with spot channels—outside the scope of a basic PMS swatch setup, but worth flagging with the printer.

**Watch for overrides:** Local formatting or **[Registration]** can sneak in. Use **Window → Output → Separations Preview** (when available) to see whether your Pantone actually separates on its own plate.

## Do not let InDesign “helpfully” convert your spot to process

Common pitfalls:

- **Transparency flattener** settings that convert spots to process during export.
- **Color conversion** at export time (for example converting everything to a single CMYK profile) without preserving spot colors.
- **Copy-paste** from RGB Illustrator or RGB PDF snippets that re-map colors into process builds.

**Mitigation:** Use a **print PDF preset** supplied by the printer, or build your own with **spot colors preserved**. When in doubt, send a **low-res draft** early and ask, **“Do you see two spots + CMYK, or only four plates?”**

## Export a PDF that preserves Pantone

Exact dialogs move between versions, but the pattern is stable:

1. **File → Adobe PDF Presets →** choose **PDF/X-1a** or the preset your vendor named (many packaging workflows still love **X-1a** for predictability).
2. In **Export Adobe PDF** options, open **Output** (or **Ink Manager** in some flows).
3. Confirm **spot colors** are **not** being converted to process unless that is intentional.
4. Use **Ink Manager** if you must remap a duplicate spot name—do that **with the printer**, not solo, so you do not collapse two different inks into one.

Export one page with a solid Pantone box and open it in **Acrobat → Print Production → Output Preview** (if available) to confirm separations.

## Handoff checklist

- Spot swatch exists, named clearly, **Color Type = Spot**.
- Printer confirmed **book** (C vs U) and **PDF preset**.
- Separations preview (or vendor feedback) shows **PMS plates** where expected.
- Internal doc lists **PMS + HEX/RGB** for digital teams—use converters above so numbers stay consistent.
